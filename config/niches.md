## 1. privacy-compliance-smb
- **Name:** Data privacy & state privacy law compliance for SMBs
- **Audience:** Operations leads, founders, and fractional compliance officers at small-to-mid businesses (10–200 employees) navigating GDPR, CCPA, and the growing patchwork of US state privacy laws without enterprise budgets
- **Why this niche:** Mandatory-buyer dynamic (regulatory, not optional), enterprise tools like OneTrust are priced out of reach for SMBs, and new state laws keep expanding the addressable market
- **Out of scope:** Enterprise-grade privacy platforms, cybersecurity tooling unrelated to privacy law, healthcare-specific HIPAA workflows (separate niche)

## 2. trades-hvac-plumbing-electrical
- **Name:** Skilled trades operators (HVAC, plumbing, electrical contractors)
- **Audience:** Owner-operators and small crews (1–15 employees) running residential and light commercial service businesses, currently using spreadsheets, paper invoices, and group texts
- **Why this niche:** Massive underserved market, willing to pay $50–$150/month, low SaaS competition because tech founders ignore them, strong recurring operational pain points
- **Out of scope:** Enterprise field service platforms (ServiceTitan tier), pure marketing/SEO tools, generic small business CRMs

## 3. private-practice-therapists
- **Name:** Solo and small-group therapists / mental health practitioners
- **Audience:** Licensed therapists, counselors, and psychologists in private practice (solo or 2–5 person groups), often cash-pay or mixed insurance, managing intake, notes, scheduling, and HIPAA compliance themselves
- **Why this niche:** High willingness to pay, HIPAA creates regulatory tailwind, fast-growing private-practice trend post-2020, and practitioners are vocal in online communities about software pain
- **Out of scope:** Large clinical organizations, telehealth platforms competing with SimplePractice/Headway, consumer-facing mental health apps

## 4. criminal-defense-discovery
- **Name:** Criminal defense attorneys (digital discovery workflows)
- **Audience:** Solo and small-firm criminal defense attorneys (1–10 attorneys) drowning in terabytes of body cam footage, dashcam video, jail calls, and cell phone extractions per case
- **Why this niche:** Brand-new problem (last 5–7 years), no incumbent has solved it well, AI transcription/search recently good enough to make it viable, and personal access through wife's law school network for validation
- **Out of scope:** General case management software (Clio/Filevine territory), civil litigation discovery, public defender office procurement (separate buying motion)

## 5. independent-insurance-agents
- **Name:** Independent insurance agents and small agencies
- **Audience:** Independent P&C and life/health agents running their own books (solo or 2–10 person agencies), managing renewals, commissions, carrier portals, and client follow-up across multiple carriers
- **Why this niche:** Vertical CRMs consistently reach $10K+ MRR, existing tools (AgencyBloc, EZLynx) are dated and expensive, agents are recurring-revenue businesses themselves so they understand subscription value
- **Out of scope:** Captive agents (Allstate/State Farm employees with corporate tools), enterprise agency management systems, consumer-facing insurance comparison sites
