> I've been signed up with lead-based platforms for about 6 months now. Like yourself, I was mainly just trying to keep my head above water starting out. Some months are okay, other months I've had to dip into savings just to cover bills and operating expenses.
>
> For me, it's not really a black-and-white issue. I don't necessarily enjoy lead-based residential service work, but right now it's the only consistent source of company revenue I have while trying to build the business.
>
> One of the frustrating parts is the way some of these platforms work. On certain sites, you're paying for leads or even paying just to bid. In some cases, the customer accepts multiple contractors at once, so you can end up getting charged a lead fee without ever even speaking to the homeowner.
>
> I also found that you end up competing heavily against handyman pricing or extremely low bids. I've had homeowners show me prices from other contractors or even screenshots of competing bids. A lot of people just go with the cheapest number, whether it's the right price or not.
